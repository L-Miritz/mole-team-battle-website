1 INTRODUCTION

Mole Team Battle is a private Mole Shopper event created by myself, Magnus. It is inspired by the Team Battle mode in Tekken fighting games as it uses the same competition structure.

2 HOW THE COMPETITION WORKS

This modality has been thought to be played with 8, 10 or 12 players, divided into 2 teams. Details of each are as follows:

8 PLAYERS
- small amount of players
- maximum of 15 games
- perfect seed balance

10 PLAYERS
- ideal amount of players
- maximum of 19 games
- imperfect seed balance

12 PLAYERS
- large amount of players
- maximum of 23 games
- perfect seed balance

For this event's first edition, the 12-player format will be used. In order for this modality to be more easily understood, it will be explained with a list of players that are bound to participate as shown below:

Each player must order the other participants from strongest (1) to weakest (11) in a rating list according to their own judgement. The host, Magnus, must have the rating list of all players, including his own. This will be used to make the two teams as balanced as possible.

Once all lists are gathered by the host, the players' rating points will be counted and published on this site so as to define the seeds, which are referenced from an ordinary single elimination tournament bracket by standard and adapted to the Team Battle modality structure. The players who were rated stronger overall will have less rating points and the smaller seed number in the Team Battle structure. In case of a draw in rating points, the players' placements will be determined by who had higher rating by the majority of the remaining players. If any draw still persists, the players will be randomly drawn.

With the seeds assigned to the players, they are placed into the Team Battle structure as shown below:

With the players having been properly divided into the two balanced teams, the two lowest seeds start playing against each other. Each player gets knocked out after losing two games. Notice that in this modality, draws count as double K.O.s, so both players lose the game. Once a player loses twice, the next one in line from their team continues. Each player keeps facing the next opponents until either all opponents are beaten twice or until the player loses two games, their two lives ( ). The teams are seeded and the lower seeds play first, so that the latter players are expected to be able to play as well. The team who eventually remains with at least one life ( ) wins the team battle, although it is worth noticing that, despite being highly unlikely, the competition may end in draw.

3.2 RULES

The rules are mostly the same traditional ones used in my 2020 Mole Shopper Tournament that was hosted at Challonge, as follows:

  • Mole Shopper Scheme: FMA's Pro Mole Shopper (information at https://www.tus-wa.com/schemes/scheme-2089/)
  • Modality: 1 vs 1
  • Worms per Team: 4
  • Hosting Channel: #RopersHeaven
  • Hosting Method: HostingBuddy (ONLY)
  • The matches must be played in Mole Shopper maps of standard size (1920 x 696 pixels) with 18 holes generated by the game, and must be agreed by the opponent.
  • To host a match, one of the players must type !host mole (for a public room) or !phost mole (for a private room) in the WormNET #RopersHeaven channel, and once inside the game room, the command !tusscheme 2089 must be typed and an agreed map loaded.
  • Notice that the command !reload must be typed if players decide to replay a same map that has just been played.
  • Before starting each game, please be sure to set the color of your team battle (either red or blue) to your worms team as this will make replay watching clearer later on.
  • For this Team Battle modality, I would like that the participating players do not spectate their teammates' games since it is 1 vs 1 and the teams are balanced already. The participating players will, however, be able to watch all games played in this event as I will be posting their replays online for download during its progress.
  • The game replays (including disconnected ones if any) must be sent to my email at kyle.madigan11@hotmail.com by at least one of the two players of each game.
  • In case of network drop/disconnection, start the match over again. The only way a disconnection may result in victory and defeat is if it is obvious in the game replay that one of the players did not have chances of winning anymore.
  • Quitting results in losing the game.
  • Disrespect with participants may result in disqualification.
  • If a player renounces the competition, their remaning lives will be lost, and the team won't be granted a player replacement, so be considerate if you do need to renounce as you would be significantly affecting your team's strength.
  • In order to participate, players must have an updated version of the game: 3.8 or higher.
  • Any doubts, talk to me in WormNET (My snooper is Magnus-snp) or send me an email at kyle.madigan11@hotmail.com
